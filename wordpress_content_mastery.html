Posts vs Pages - Understanding the Foundation

The Newspaper vs Business Card Analogy

WordPress has two main content types: posts and pages. Think of posts like newspaper articles - they're timely, part of an ongoing conversation, organized chronologically, and people expect them regularly. Pages are like business cards or brochures - they contain essential information that doesn't change often and represents your core identity.

Content Organization and Taxonomy

Categories vs Tags - The Filing System

Think of categories as the filing cabinets in your office, and tags as the sticky notes you put on documents. Categories are broad, hierarchical groupings (like "Marketing" with subcategories "Social Media" and "Email Marketing"), while tags are specific keywords that describe the content (like "Facebook ads," "email automation," or "conversion rates").

SEO-Optimized Content Creation

Writing for Humans and Search Engines

SEO writing is like being a translator between humans and robots. You need to create content that engages human readers while also speaking the language that search engines understand. The best SEO content doesn't feel "SEO-optimized" to readers - it feels natural and helpful.

The SEO Content Checklist

Pre-Publishing SEO Checklist:

Title Optimization:
□ Primary keyword in title (preferably at beginning)
□ Title under 60 characters
□ Compelling and click-worthy
□ Unique across your site

Content Structure:
□ Only one H1 tag (usually the title)
□ Logical heading hierarchy (H2, H3, etc.)
□ Primary keyword in first paragraph
□ Keywords naturally integrated throughout
□ Content answers the search query completely

Technical Elements:
□ Meta description written (150-160 characters)
□ Image alt text includes relevant keywords
□ Internal links to related content (3-5 per post)
□ External links to authority sources (2-3 per post)
□ URL is short and keyword-friendly

Content Quality:
□ Minimum 300 words (longer for competitive topics)
□ Original, valuable content
□ Proper grammar and spelling
□ Mobile-friendly formatting
□ Fast loading images (compressed)

User Experience:
□ Clear call-to-action
□ Easy to read and scan
□ Engaging introduction
□ Satisfying conclusion
□ Social sharing buttons available

Working with the WordPress REST API for Content

WordPress REST API - Content Endpoints:

Basic Content Retrieval:
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/posts          # Get all posts
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/posts/123      # Get specific post
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/pages          # Get all pages
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/categories     # Get categories
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/tags           # Get tags

Advanced Content Queries:
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/posts?per_page=5&orderby=date&order=desc
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/posts?categories=10&tags=15
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/posts?search=wordpress&_fields=id,title,excerpt
GET /wp-json/wp/v2/posts?author=2&status=publish

Comprehensive Content Backup Strategy:

Automated Backups:
□ Daily database backups (content and settings)
□ Weekly full site backups (files + database)
□ Real-time backup for high-traffic sites
□ Automated off-site storage (cloud services)

Version Control for Content:
□ Use revision history in WordPress
□ Export content regularly as XML

□ Keep archives of important content versions

Multiple Storage Locations:
